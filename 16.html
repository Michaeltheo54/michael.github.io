22 things you didn't know about football (soccer)

Football is the most popular sport in the world. There are more than 265 million players worldwide and around 4 billion people regularly follow this sport, making it certainly the most popular sport in the world.
England invented football as we know it today, although the sport's origins are in China. The first rules were decided in 1863 and many changes and additions have been made since then.

In this article, i'm going to be revealing 22 things you didn't know about football.
1. The fastest goal ever scored was after 2.4 seconds.
Nawaf Al-Abed scored after just 2.4 seconds and is often considered the fastest goal ever scored. There are many examples of players scoring in the first 5 seconds.

2. Most footballs are produced in Pakistan.
Sialkot, Pakistan is famous for producing FIFA World Cup balls since 1982. And some of the most beautiful balls are made by hand stitching in Pakistan. For example, Adidas soccer balls are produced here as well as other high-quality soccer balls.

3. Only 8 countries have won the World Cup
The FIFA World Cup has been held every four years since 1930, and although there have been 21 tournaments since the first one in Uruguay, there have only been 8 countries who have won this tournament. Brazil have won the FIFA World Cup five times, making them the most successful national team of all time.

4. Americans and Canadians call it soccer
When an American talks about football, they usually refer to American football, one of the most popular sports in the United States. Football in Canada refers to Canadian football, very similar to the American version. Canadians and Americans are the only people in the world who call it soccer rather than football.
There are many other variations of football. The most common version is actually called Association Football, but it is almost always known by Americans and Canadians simply as football, or soccer a term first introduced in England. Some other variations of this sport include futsal, eFootball and beach football.

5. Football players can receive yellow cards and red cards
Players can receive yellow cards or red cards from the referee if they violate any rules of the match. For example, this could be a rough tackle or saying something inappropriate to the referee. If a player receives 2 yellow cards in the same match, he will receive a red card and be sent off.

6. The first international match took place in 1872.
The first official international football match took place at Hamilton Crescent, where Scotland played against England. It took place on November 30, 1872 and a total of 114 matches have taken place since then between the two national teams.

7. The largest football stadium is in North Korea.
May Day Rungrado Stadium in Pyongyang is the largest stadium in the world with a capacity of up to 150,000 people. Some sources claim that the stadium only has a capacity of 114,000 people, still placing it in first place among the largest stadiums. However, the largest attendance ever recorded was at the Maracanã Stadium in 1950, where 199,854 people watched Uraguay's match against Brazil. Current attendance is 78,838 people.

8. Kazuyoshi Miura is the oldest professional soccer player
Japanese soccer player and icon Kazuyoshi Miura, also known as Kazu, is currently the oldest professional soccer player of all time. He is over 50 years old and broke the previous record held by Sir Stanley Mathews at the age of 50. Kazuyoshi Miura plays in the Japanese championship for Yokohama FC.

9. Norway is the only national team in the world that has never lost to Brazil.

10. India withdrew from the 1950 FIFA World Cup because they were not allowed to play barefoot.

11. An average footballer runs 9.65 km per match.

12. Cristiano Ronaldo is the only player to have scored in every minute from 1 to 90 minutes.

13. The first FIFA World Cup was held in Uraguay, the team also won the competition.

14. The heaviest defeat in football history of all time took place in 1885, when Bon Accord lost to Arbroath with a score of 0-36.

15. The player who scores the most goals in a match is Archie Thompson. He scored 13 goals for the Socceroos as Australia beat American Samoa 31-0.

16. Until the late 1960s, red and yellow cards did not exist.
Previously, football referees had to record everything in a book. After analyzing a number of scandals that occurred in international matches related to language barriers between referees and players, Ken Aston, president of the jury at the World Championship, decided to make the penalty system more visible. He made such a decision in his mind while driving and stopped at a traffic light. When he returned home, he shared his idea with his wife, who immediately turned his idea into reality by cutting red and yellow cards from colored cardboard.

17. Soccer fields have stripes
This is not because they are planted with different types of grass, but thanks to the work of lawn mowers. Mowers are masters at creating the illusion of stripes. If the grass slopes away from the viewer, the lawn appears lighter due to the higher area of reflection from whole stems, and vice versa. Such a field color is not only beautiful but also helps the referee detect offsides.

18. Some soccer goalkeepers score their own goals
They not only help their team by preventing opposing players from scoring goals but also by taking penalties and freely kicking and scoring goals.
This is especially common among South American goalkeepers. The best goalkeeper is Brazilian Rogerio Ceni, who has scored more than 100 goals. Paraguayan extravagant goalkeeper José Luis Chilavert is also well known. He scored 62 goals but is still the only goalkeeper to score a hat-trick in a match.

19. László Kubala is the only player to have played for three countries
Born to Czech parents in Budapest, Barcelona icon Kubala played for Czechoslovakia, Hungary and Spain. Former Real Madrid striker Alfredo Di Stefano played for Argentina, Spain and Colombia. However, his time playing for Colombia in the early 1950s took place at a time when the country's football federation was not officially recognized by FIFA.

20. Zlatan Ibrahimovic has played for six Champions League winning clubs, but he has never won the title himself

21. More than 5 billion people watched the 2022 FIFA World Cup
The FIFA World Cup is the most watched tournament and sporting event and the number of people that watched the 2022 World Cup was estimated at over 5 billion people, which is a new record. Also, an estimated 1.5 billion people watched the 2022 FIFA World Cup final between France and Argentina, which Argentina won.

22. Sheffield F.C. is the world's oldest football club
Sheffield F.C is the world's oldest professional football club and was founded in 1857. The club currently plays in the Northern Premier League Division One South East.
